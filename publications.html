A Cluster-based Networking Approach for Large-Scale and Wide-Area Quantum Key Agreement
Zhonghui Li, Kaiping Xue*, Qidong Jia, Jian Li, David S. L. Wei, Jianqing Liu, Nenghai Yu
Quantum Information Processing, vol. 21, no. 192, 2022 Springer
Graphical Abstract

Quantum key distribution (QKD) is a cryptographic technique for generating random secure keys, theoretically enabling unconditionally secure remote classical communication. However, QKD technology currently faces two core problems: extending key distribution distances and achieving concurrent key agreements between multiple pairs of QKD nodes. This paper proposes an innovative cluster-based QKD network structure consisting of QKD nodes grouped into multiple clusters, a backbone network connecting the clusters, and quantum repeaters interconnected to form a mesh network as the QKD backbone.

An Improved QKD Protocol without Public Announcement Basis using Periodically Derived Basis
Qidong Jia, Kaiping Xue*, Zhonghui Li, Mengce Zheng, David S. L. Wei, Nenghai Yu
Quantum Information Processing, vol. 20, no. 69, 2021 Springer
Graphical Abstract

The quantum key distribution (QKD) protocol provides an absolutely secure way to distribute secret keys, where security can be guaranteed by quantum mechanics. To raise the key generation rate of classical BB84 QKD protocol, Hwang et al. proposed a subtle variation in which a pre-shared secret string is used to generate the consistent basis. In this work, we propose a simple attack strategy to obtain all preparation basis by stealing partial information in each round. To eliminate this security threat, we further propose an improved QKD protocol using the idea of iteratively updating the basis.
